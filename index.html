TM4C Maze Game

On the left you can see a toddler playing this game using the two onboard buttons. Eventually they were able to figure out that one button was used for movement and the other was for rotating the intended direction. Not long after, they were able to complete the maze with ease.
Graphics for this game were manually implemented. For example the triangle used to show the direction of movement is created by a function which draws three lines about a specified origin. To fill the triangle the lines are drawn again with a continuously shrinking radius.

Wooden Electric skateboard

This was designed for fun but later became somewhat necessary for moving around UCLA campus after a fifth metatarsal fracture. Initially the wheels used a small bike chain with a sprocket that was CAD designed and CNC cut for the specific wheel it was placed on. This was later changed to a 3D printed PLA, and later water-sliced aluminum timing belt pulley.
The wooden battery and VESC enclosures were designed and then Laser Cut with 3D printed L-brackets. Lastly the motors are protected by a designed 3D printed part.
